# 🛍️ Retail Sales Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a retail sales dataset to identify customer purchasing behavior, sales trends, product performance, and revenue patterns. The objective is to derive meaningful business insights through data visualization and provide actionable recommendations that can support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze monthly and quarterly sales trends.
- Understand customer demographics based on age and gender.
- Identify the best-performing product categories.
- Analyze revenue generated across product categories.
- Explore relationships between numerical variables.
- Provide business recommendations based on analytical findings.

---

## 📂 Dataset Information

The dataset contains retail transaction records with the following attributes:

| Column Name | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Transaction ID | Unique transaction identifier |
| Date | Date of transaction |
| Customer ID | Unique customer identifier |
| Gender | Customer gender |
| Age | Customer age |
| Product Category | Category of purchased product |
| Quantity | Number of units purchased |
| Price per Unit | Price of each product |
| Total Amount | Total transaction value |

## 🧹 Data Preparation

The following preprocessing steps were performed before analysis:

- Converted the **Date** column to datetime format.
- Created **Age Group** categories from the Age column.
- Generated **Year-Month** and **Quarter** columns for trend analysis.
- Checked the dataset for missing values and inconsistencies.
- Prepared numerical features for correlation analysis.

# 📈 Key Insights

- The highest monthly sales were recorded in **May 2023**, while the lowest occurred in **January 2024**.
- Sales peaked during **Q4 of 2023**, whereas **Q1 of 2024** recorded the lowest quarterly sales.
- Customers aged **46–55 years** represent the largest customer segment.
- The **46–55 age group** contributes the highest share of total sales.
- **Female customers** generate higher overall revenue than male customers.
- **Electronics** contributes the highest total revenue among all product categories.
- **Clothing** records the highest quantity of products sold.
- The correlation analysis shows:
  - **Total Amount** has a strong positive correlation with **Quantity** and **Price per Unit**.
  - **Age** has little to no correlation with sales-related variables.
  - **Quantity** and **Price per Unit** exhibit only a weak relationship, indicating that higher-priced products are not necessarily purchased in larger quantities.

---

# 💡 Business Recommendations

### 1. Target High-Value Customer Segments

Customers aged **46–55** contribute the highest revenue. Personalized marketing campaigns, loyalty programs, and exclusive offers should be designed to improve customer retention within this segment.

### 2. Prioritize High-Revenue Product Categories

Although Clothing products sell in larger quantities, the **Electronics** category generates the highest revenue. Increasing promotional efforts and maintaining sufficient inventory for Electronics can improve overall profitability.

### 3. Optimize Inventory for Seasonal Demand

Sales performance peaks during **Q4** and **May**, indicating seasonal demand patterns. Inventory planning and promotional campaigns should be aligned with these peak periods while introducing offers during slower months to improve sales consistency.

### 4. Improve Product-Level Data Collection

The dataset only contains product categories. Recording individual product names or IDs would enable deeper analysis, including top-selling products, customer preferences, and inventory optimization.

---

# ✅ Conclusion

The analysis highlights important trends in customer purchasing behavior, seasonal sales, and product category performance. Customers aged **46–55** and female shoppers contribute significantly to overall revenue, while the Electronics category generates the highest sales value despite Clothing recording the highest sales volume. Seasonal peaks observed during **Q4** emphasize the importance of strategic inventory management and targeted marketing initiatives. These insights can help retailers improve operational efficiency, maximize revenue, and make informed business decisions.
